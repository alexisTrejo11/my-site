# High Availability

> HA — designing for continued service through component failure.

## What it is

- Eliminate single points of failure (multi-AZ, replicas).
- Measure uptime SLOs (99.9%, 99.99%).
- HA ≠ zero data loss — define both.

## Why it matters

Understanding **High Availability** helps you reason about trade-offs in production data systems — when to apply it, what breaks at scale, and how it connects to neighboring ideas in this section.

## In practice

- Map the concept to tools you use (PostgreSQL, Kafka, Redis, etc.).
- Look for metrics or symptoms that indicate misuse or missing optimization.
